The Register-Guard

Weekend

By Christine Sherk | Photos by Collin Andrew | The Register-Guard
Sept. 29, 2016

Quite suddenly this past weekend, it dawned on my mother and me that we had not gone to the local farms north of Eugene all summer. Not once.

This was something we had done fairly regularly through the years, first as an outing to entertain my son when he was young, and then, when he’d grown up, as a shopping trip for us to gather ingredients for our favorite harvest dish, ratatouille.

But things have slowed down on our weekend get-togethers in the past year, especially since Mom had hip surgery last fall. We stay indoors and talk more, or we work on projects. When we do go out, we are focused and stick close to home.

All you need is a glorious fall day, however, to feel the harvest pull. We grabbed our market baskets, made our mental shopping lists and drove into the country. The farms were ready for us, as they will be throughout October with a variety of what’s popularly referred to as agritainment, from delicious treats to seasonal décor, from pumpkin launching to corn mazes. (Northern Lights farm in Pleasant Hill has a haunted and Harry Potter-themed maze on Saturday nights. Lumos!)

No, we didn’t take a ride to the U-pick pumpkin patch or feed the animals, but we did snag a still-warm apple turnover — that’s lunch! — from the pastry case. We companionably gathered bodacious corn, giant peppers, kingly ‘Honeycrisp’ apples and red ripe tomatoes. Eggplant, onions — down our lists we went, even adding to the cart two pumpkins.

And then, on a lark we wandered into a U-cut flower patch of zinnias. The colors so delighted Mom, I went in search of scissors, finally taking the only pair from the checkout stand. “I’ll bring it back,” I said to the cashier, which I did, but not before Mom and I had slowed down time to a beautiful crawl through the flower rows, snipping here, snipping there, until we both held a bunch of fall in our hands.
